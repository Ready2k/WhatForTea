💡 Recommendation — ship A + C together, add B's search as a follow-up
Concept A fixes the core problem: a flat list with no hierarchy. The "Use It Up" swimlane surfaces the most urgent items without the user hunting, "Needs Confirming" makes the confidence mechanic legible to non-technical users, and the collapsible category sections scale gracefully from 5 to 50 items.

Concept C's bottom sheet applies to both A and B and is the single highest-ROI change — replacing the in-page form that currently pushes the entire list out of view.

Concept B's search + chips are worth adding once the list grows beyond ~30 items. They're additive and don't require restructuring A.

One open question: should "Confirm" remain per-item, or should there be a post-shopping bulk flow ("I just restocked — confirm everything low")? A bulk confirm in the sheet would replace dozens of individual taps and map to the real-world use case.
