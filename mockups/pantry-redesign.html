Concept A — What's new
✓Status strip — 3 at-a-glance stats replace the banner. No buried warnings.
✓"Use It Up" scroll — expiring items are a swimlane, not a wall of text in a banner.
✓"Needs Confirming" — low-confidence items get their own section with a "Confirm all" CTA.
✓Category sections — collapsible so power users can navigate quickly.
✓Compact rows — confidence dot replaces full-width bar. 2× more items visible without scrolling.
→
Concept B — What's new
✓Search bar — find any item instantly. No scrolling.
✓Filter chips — category or status filter with one tap. Works like the recipe screen.
✓Left accent bar — red/amber/green status stripe is scannable without reading numbers.
✓Auto-sorted — attention items always bubble to top, no manual hunting.
✓Swipe to edit — quantity/remove via swipe gesture, cleaner than in-row buttons.
→Simpler to build. Best if the pantry stays under ~30 items.
Concept C — Add Item Sheet
✓Bottom sheet — replaces the inline form that pushes everything down. More native-feeling.
✓Search / Scan toggle — two entry modes surfaced without a separate button in the header.
✓Expiry preview — tells you right away if the item will enter "Use It Up" after adding.
✓Confidence tooltip — inline note explains what confidence means without cluttering the list.
→This is a shared improvement — works with either A or B above.
💡 Recommendation
Ship Concept A + C together. Concept A gives the pantry genuine structure and surfaces the right information first (expiring → low confidence → everything else). Concept C's bottom sheet makes adding items feel like a native app rather than a web form. Concept B's search bar and filter chips are worth adding as a follow-up once the list grows — they're independent of the structure changes.

The one clarifying question: do you want "Confirm" to stay as a per-item button, or should confirming be more of a "I've restocked" bulk flow? If users mostly confirm after a grocery run, a bulk "Confirm after shopping" mode (mark all low-confidence items at once) might replace dozens of individual taps.
